# Advocedu Baseline Research Memo (Run: 2026-03-25T02-43-07Z)

## Executive summary
This baseline run established a credible initial evidence foundation for Advocedu on three fronts:
1. IDEA-served population growth is substantial over the last decade.
2. FERPA/HIPAA boundary supports the core records-handling model directionally.
3. Competitor pricing evidence confirms low-cost/free AI alternatives in adjacent segments.

However, two critical areas remain incomplete for investor-grade confidence:
- state-by-state dispute trend quantification from primary sources
- direct corroboration of one top-line population figure captured only via snippet in this run

## What is strong enough today
- NCES evidence shows IDEA-served population growth from 6.4M (2012-13) to 7.5M (2022-23).
- Government guidance aligns that student education records are governed by FERPA and excluded from HIPAA Privacy Rule coverage.
- MagicSchool pricing confirms active freemium/low-price competitive pressure in education AI.

## What is not yet strong enough
- National/state dispute trend claims are not yet backed by enough primary datasets in this run.
- Any absolute legal messaging (e.g., "no district consent needed" style language) needs legal review and softer wording.

## Recommendations before external use
1. Complete a dispute-data primary-source pull for Tier 1 states.
2. Corroborate the 2023-24 IDEA top-line figure from direct ED/NCES source access.
3. Keep investor-facing summary restricted to verified high-confidence claims.
